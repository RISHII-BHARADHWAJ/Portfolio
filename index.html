About Me

I am a Computer Science undergraduate specializing in Cybersecurity with a strong interest in offensive security, penetration testing, web application security, and vulnerability research. I enjoy understanding how systems fail, identifying security flaws, and assessing applications through practical security testing.

My learning focuses on Linux, networking, Active Directory, privilege escalation, and Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges, utilizing hands-on labs to practice real-world attack methodologies. At the same time, I study defensive concepts including security monitoring, SIEM tool analysis, and incident response to gain a complete, well-rounded understanding of cybersecurity.

I continuously improve my skills by reproducing attack chains, studying vulnerabilities, and developing practical projects. By combining offensive exploration with a solid understanding of how detection and mitigations are implemented, I aim to build and advocate for robust, resilient systems.
